# DeciRepo Category Definition v0.1

**Category Name:** Federated Decision Infrastructure (FDI)

---

## 1. What is FDI?
Federated Decision Infrastructure is a new class of technology designed to record, verify, and investigate institutional decisions as deterministic digital artifacts. 

Unlike traditional "Decision Support Systems" (which help humans choose) or "Workflow Engines" (который управляют процессом), FDI focuses exclusively on the **verifiability and reproducibility** of the outcome.

## 2. Core Components of FDI
- **Deterministic Execution:** The ability to recompute a decision from its inputs and rules.
- **Artifact Identity:** Cryptographic binding of data, policy, and result.
- **Independent Verification:** A network of nodes that can validate artifacts without trusting the origin.
- **Investigation Layer:** Structured evidence chains (precedents, explanations, divergence).

## 3. Why Now?
The market currently has a "Verifiability Gap." Decisions are stored in opaque silos (logs, emails, Slack). As institutional complexity grows, the cost of audit and the risk of "black box" decisions become unsustainable. FDI closes this gap by making decisions as verifiable as code commits.

## 4. Market Position
FDI sits at the intersection of **Compliance, Cybersecurity, and Governance**. It is the infrastructure layer that enables "Trust through Proof."
