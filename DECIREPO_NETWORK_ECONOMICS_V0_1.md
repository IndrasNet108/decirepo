# DeciRepo Network Economics v0.1

**Status:** Draft Economic Specification  
**Objective:** Sustainable Federated Verification Infrastructure

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the economic model for DeciRepo. The purpose is to ensure the network sustains artifact publication, independent verification, storage, and stewardship while preserving architectural integrity.

**Core Constraint:** DeciRepo economics MUST NOT depend on speculative tokens, artificial scarcity, or monetization of unverifiable claims.

---

## 2. Core Principle: The Verification Asymmetry

The network follows a fundamental rule:

**Write is paid. Verification is open or near-free. Assurance is premium.**

- **Publishing** decision artifacts is a paid operation (covers storage and ingestion costs).
- **Basic verification** access remains open or low-cost to ensure public legitimacy.
- **High-assurance** services (SLA, certified audits, priority rebuilds) are monetized to incentivize operators.

---

## 3. Economic Roles

### 3.1 Issuer
The organization producing decision artifacts. Pays for publication, storage, and evidence bundle generation.

### 3.2 Verifier Operator
Independent node operator. Compensated through direct commercial contracts, managed verification subscriptions, or assurance service fees.

### 3.3 Steward (IndrasNet OÜ)
Responsible for the reference node and conformance. Funded through commercial managed services, certification fees, and membership.

### 3.4 Auditor / Investigator
Consumes verification outputs. Pays for bulk access, premium investigation tools, and historical evidence retention.

---

## 4. Cost Surfaces

1. **Publication Cost:** Ingestion, canonicalization, and identity generation.
2. **Verification Cost:** Retrieval, deterministic rebuild, and result publication.
3. **Storage Cost:** Decision and evidence artifact retention.
4. **Network Cost:** Node discovery and API serving.
5. **Stewardship Cost:** Protocol maintenance and conformance updates.

---

## 5. Operator Incentives

Operators are incentivized to provide high-quality verification capacity through:
- **Commercial Contracts:** Direct B2B agreements for verification services.
- **Assurance Revenue:** Fees for providing certified, high-reliability rebuilds.
- **Federation Participation:** Agreements between registries to mutually verify each other's data.

The network rewards **availability, rebuild reliability, and evidence completeness**.

---

## 6. Summary

DeciRepo economics are designed to sustain a verification infrastructure, not to financialize it. By ensuring that those who benefit from verifiable records (Issuers) pay for their creation, and those who need certainty (Auditors) pay for assurance, the network builds a stable, long-term foundation for institutional trust.
