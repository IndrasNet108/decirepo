The point where wording becomes binding or fails.

Because one sentence can create liability.

These are not examples.

These are moments where the reviewed version and the sent version became different positions.

Case 01 · Legal Outbound Drift

Reviewed:
"We are reviewing the incident and will respond after completing our internal assessment. All rights are reserved."

Sent:
"We confirm the incident falls under our responsibility and will arrange settlement after receiving the supporting documents."
→ This is where liability was created.

The reviewed version was neutral. The outbound version created both an admission and a commitment.

Case 02 · Commercial Commitment Drift

Reviewed:
"We are still evaluating the scope and will confirm once internal alignment is complete."

Sent:
"We are aligned and will proceed with the agreed scope and timeline."
→ This is where commitment was created before execution.

Negotiation was still open. The outbound message closed it.

Case 03 · Outbound Position Drift

Reviewed:
"We acknowledge receipt of your request and will revert after internal review."

Sent:
"We will proceed with settlement upon receiving the required documentation."
→ This is where the company took a position it never approved.

Acknowledgment turned into commitment. No formal approval ever happened.

Before It Is Sent

Before a message is sent, it becomes a position.

If the reviewed version and the outbound wording are not the same position, the transition should not proceed.
