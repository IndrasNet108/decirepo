Public Challenge Cases

Bounded wording-drift cases for fail-closed control claims

Boundary Under Test

These cases test whether a reviewed internal state and a final outbound commitment still align at the moment of commit.

Problem class
Semantic drift between reviewed wording and effective external commitment.
Not in scope
Engine internals, extraction logic, hashing strategy, or implementation-specific control paths.
Question
Would the transition still proceed, or fail closed, if the outbound wording no longer matches what was actually reviewed?

Case 01 · Legal Outbound Drift

A neutral reviewed draft shifts into language that can be read as both admission and commitment.

Reviewed draft:
"We are reviewing the incident and will respond after completing our internal assessment. All rights are reserved."

Modified version before send:
"We confirm the incident falls under our responsibility and will arrange settlement after receiving the supporting documents."
Reviewed state
Neutral correspondence posture.
Outbound effect
Can be read as creating both an admission and a commitment.
Test question
Would this transition be allowed after human confirmation, or fail unless the reviewed version and outbound wording still align at commit?

Case 02 · Commercial Commitment Drift

A negotiation-safe draft shifts into wording that can be read as commercial acceptance before formal execution.

Reviewed draft:
"We have received your proposal and will revert after internal review. Nothing in this message should be treated as final acceptance."

Modified version before send:
"We are aligned on the commercial terms and can proceed on that basis once the paperwork is completed."
Reviewed state
Negotiation posture remains open.
Outbound effect
Can be read as commercial acceptance before formal execution.
Test question
Would this transition be allowed after human confirmation, or fail unless the reviewed version and outbound wording still align at commit?

How To Use These Cases

These are challenge cases, not product documentation. They are designed to test whether a claimed fail-closed architecture preserves alignment between reviewed wording and the effective outbound position at the moment of commit.
